About Me

I am a Computer Science undergraduate with a strong curiosity for understanding how computing systems work beneath the surface. Rather than focusing on a single role or technology, I am interested in building solid fundamentals across core computer science domains.

My interest in cybersecurity emerged as a natural extension of this curiosity. Studying security has helped me understand systems, networks, and software behavior from a deeper perspective — including how assumptions break, how failures occur, and how systems can be designed more robustly.

I view cybersecurity not as an isolated career path, but as a lens through which broader computer science concepts such as operating systems, networking, cryptography, and software engineering can be explored and understood more deeply.

Alongside security-focused exploration, I am equally interested in programming, problem-solving, and system-level concepts. My goal is to continuously grow my understanding of computer science as a discipline, keeping myself open to software engineering, systems roles, and research-oriented opportunities.

This website is designed to reflect that learning journey — documenting areas I have explored, areas I am currently focusing on, and areas I am curious to explore further, without locking myself into fixed labels or premature specialization.
